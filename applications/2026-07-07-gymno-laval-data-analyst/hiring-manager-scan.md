## Will It Make an Impact? **Yes**

## Why or Why Not

### Does the top of the resume immediately convey relevance to the JD?
Yes. Within the top third, the manager sees (a) a Summary that names the exact stack the JD asks for, (b) a **Master of Professional Studies in Data Analytics** — which more than satisfies the "related degree preferred," and (c) a current **Operations Data Assistant** role whose first bullet is a Power BI + DAX dashboard win. For a role that opens with "Build and maintain dashboards and reports (Excel, SQL, Tableau/Power BI)," that is a bullseye in the first few seconds.

### Do the first visible bullets show seniority + impact + JD-aligned skills?
- **Seniority/scope:** Appropriate for the role's target ("early in their data career or looking to grow") — the JD explicitly welcomes emerging analysts, and this candidate reads as slightly above entry-level with real ownership (managed a 1,000+ record database).
- **Quantified impact:** Yes — 13% refresh-time cut, thousands in annual savings, 1,000+ records managed. Every top bullet carries a number.
- **Skill alignment:** Power BI, DAX, SQL, dashboards, data integrity — all in the top third and all JD-named.

### Is the candidate's "story" clear in 10 seconds?
Yes. The story is: "Data Analytics master's + a current operations-data role doing Power BI/SQL reporting, backed by analytics projects." That trajectory matches a growing analyst exactly what the JD wants.

### Red flags the hiring manager would catch
- **Two of three experience entries are titled "Software Developer."** A skeptical manager might read "engineer pivoting to analytics." Mitigated by the Summary, the Data Analytics degree, and the analytics-framed project section — but it is the one thing that could give pause.
- **Vault AI listed as "Remote" with a ~6-month tenure.** Short stint; unlikely to sink the read but could prompt a question.
- No employment-gap or formatting red flags.

## Specific Fixes to Maximize 10-Second Impact

1. **Keep the Operations Data Assistant role and Summary in the top third** (they already are) — this is what converts the "developer?" doubt into "analyst." Do not let Experience get pushed below the fold by an overlong summary.
2. **Consider leading the Summary with the degree + current data role** so "Data Analytics" is the very first substantive phrase the eye lands on (it currently leads with "data analytics professional," which is close — minor).
3. Optional: land a literal "Analyzed..." verb in the project section to mirror the JD's opening responsibility verb.

**Overall:** The top third makes a strong, role-relevant impact. This resume earns a "read deeper," not a reject.
